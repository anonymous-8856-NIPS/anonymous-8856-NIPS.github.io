From Oracle Fragments to a Continuous Manifold

Of the approximately 4,500 Oracle Bone Inscription characters discovered from the Shang dynasty, only about 1,600 have been deciphered. Existing methods often compare OBI with one later period independently, but many characters change non-monotonically across intermediate dynasties. The project page now introduces the argument as a short textual chain rather than four separate boxes.

01
Start from the failure of single-dynasty matching.

Direct OBI-to-modern or OBI-to-one-era comparison can miss characters whose forms shift sharply in Bronze, Seal, or Clerical stages. The central task is therefore not static image matching, but recovering a historically coherent trajectory.

02
Use pre-diagnosis to justify the method before presenting it.

The paper first diagnoses missing intermediate stages, fragmented evolution chains, non-monotone morphological shifts, and rule-system explosion. This motivates a continuous formulation rather than another isolated retrieval or generation module.

03
Model script history as a continuous manifold flow.

MSEF represents each glyph as an era-specific manifold point and learns inter-era transition dynamics with Neural ODEs, so partial cross-era pairs can still contribute supervision even when complete five-era chains are scarce.

04
Verify decipherment candidates through forward recall and backward consistency.

CBED first recalls candidates along the learned historical flow, applies survival-aware pruning, and then traces candidates backward to reject paths that are visually plausible but evolutionarily inconsistent.

05
Use post-analysis to show what the model learned.

Mechanistic visualizations, attention circuits, saliency tests, velocity topology, and qualitative retrieval figures should appear later in the page as evidence that MSEF captures paleographic structure rather than shallow pixel similarity.
