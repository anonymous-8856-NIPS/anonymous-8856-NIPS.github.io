Core narrative

From Fragmented Glyphs to a Continuous Evolution Model

The page now follows the same logic as the manuscript: problem, data, model, inference, and evidence. Each section has one job.

Oracle Bone decipherment is not just a visual matching problem. A glyph may look very different across Bronze, Seal, Clerical, and modern scripts while still belonging to one historical trajectory. The central idea of MSEF is to model that trajectory directly instead of comparing isolated snapshots.

  1. 01Why direct matching fails. Intermediate dynasties can introduce sharp visual shifts, so nearest-neighbor similarity against one later period is often misleading.
  2. 02Why the dataset matters. Complete five-era chains are scarce, but partial cross-era correspondences are abundant and can supervise continuous dynamics.
  3. 03What MSEF learns. Each character is represented as an era-specific manifold point, and Neural ODEs learn how points move between eras.
  4. 04How inference works. CBED recalls candidates forward, removes unlikely or extinct paths, and checks final candidates by tracing them backward.
  5. 05Why the result is inspectable. Post-analysis examines attention, saliency, topology, and variant networks to test whether the model learned real paleographic structure.

Dataset

Fine-Grained Cross-Era Supervision

The dataset is not a side product. It is what lets MSEF learn from incomplete historical chains rather than requiring every character to survive through all eras.

The dataset preserves era, subperiod, regional, visual, structural, semantic, contextual, and spatiotemporal signals. This lets the model use many partial correspondences rather than only the small number of complete OBI-to-modern chains.

  1. 01Cross-era coverage. Characters are linked across OBI, Bronze, Seal, Clerical, and Regular scripts when evidence is available.
  2. 02Fine-grained time. OBI groups and Bronze subperiods preserve within-era variation instead of collapsing all history into coarse labels.
  3. 03Partial chains as signal. Non-complete pairs become useful training evidence under a continuous manifold formulation.

Method

Pre-Diagnosis → MSEF → CBED → Post-Analysis

The method is presented as a pipeline: diagnose the failure mode, learn continuous dynamics, verify candidates bidirectionally, then inspect the learned mechanisms.

01

Pre-diagnosis

Identify why direct generation, retrieval, and rule enumeration break under missing intermediate evidence.

02

Continuous manifold

Represent each glyph as an era-conditioned point and learn inter-era movement with Neural ODEs.

03

CBED inference

Recall candidates forward, prune implausible or extinct paths, and verify cycle consistency backward.

04

Post-analysis

Use mechanistic visualizations to test whether the model learns paleographic structure.

Evidence

Manuscript Figures, Organized by Argument

These figures are not a decorative gallery. Each group answers one question a reader is likely to ask while reading the paper.

1. Why is this method necessary?

Pre-exploratory diagnosis

The pre-analysis figures show why the method is derived from the structure of the problem: characters preserve latent identity while their visible forms branch, simplify, and reorganize across time.

2. How does inference avoid false matches?

CBED inference

The inference figures explain how forward recall alone can be too permissive, and why survival-aware pruning plus backward verification is needed.

3. What does the model learn internally?

Mechanistic post-analysis

The post-analysis figures make the learned representation inspectable through attention, saliency, topology, causal intervention, and vector arithmetic.
