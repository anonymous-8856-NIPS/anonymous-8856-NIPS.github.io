From Oracle Fragments to a Continuous Manifold

Of the approximately 4,500 Oracle Bone Inscription characters discovered from the Shang dynasty, only about 1,600 have been deciphered. Existing methods often compare OBI with one later period independently, but many characters change non-monotonically across intermediate dynasties. This page presents the method as one continuous story, not as disconnected visual modules.

01
Start from the failure of single-dynasty matching.

Direct OBI-to-modern or OBI-to-one-era comparison can miss characters whose forms shift sharply in Bronze, Seal, or Clerical stages. The central task is therefore not static image matching, but recovering a historically coherent trajectory.

02
Use pre-diagnosis to justify the method before presenting it.

The pre-analysis diagnoses missing intermediate stages, fragmented evolution chains, non-monotone morphological shifts, and rule-system explosion. This motivates a continuous formulation rather than another isolated retrieval or generation module.

03
Model script history as a continuous manifold flow.

MSEF represents each glyph as an era-specific manifold point and learns inter-era transition dynamics with Neural ODEs, so partial cross-era pairs can still contribute supervision even when complete five-era chains are scarce.

04
Verify candidates through forward recall and backward consistency.

CBED recalls candidates along the learned historical flow, applies survival-aware pruning, and traces candidates backward to reject paths that are visually plausible but evolutionarily inconsistent.

05
Use post-analysis to show what the model learned.

Mechanistic visualizations, attention circuits, saliency tests, velocity topology, and qualitative retrieval evidence are organized later as proof that MSEF captures paleographic structure rather than shallow pixel similarity.

Fine-Grained Cross-Era Dataset

The dataset is the bridge between the paleographic problem and the MSEF formulation. Instead of treating each era as one static reference set, the page explains how incomplete cross-era evidence becomes usable supervision for continuous script evolution.

Why the data matters

Oracle decipherment has a structural data bottleneck: many characters are known only through fragments or partial historical chains. MSEF uses complete five-era chains when available, but it can also learn from pairwise correspondences across any two eras.

  • 01Cross-era coverage. The visual story spans Oracle Bone, Bronze, Seal, Clerical, and Regular forms rather than forcing a direct OBI-to-modern jump.
  • 02Fine-grained time. OBI scribal groups and Bronze subperiods preserve within-era variation, so evolution is not collapsed into one representative glyph per dynasty.
  • 03Partial chains become training signal. Non-complete pairs can supervise inter-era flow, which is exactly why a continuous manifold formulation is useful.
